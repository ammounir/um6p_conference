Introduction

  • ESG is now a core investment factor alongside return and risk in both practice and research.
  • Academic focus: the trade-off between return, risk, and ESG — improving one often comes at the cost of another (Pedersen et al., 2021; Burchi & Włodarczyk, 2020; Cesarone et al., 2022; Steuer & Utz, 2023).
  • Objective: Develop a curvature-based measure to capture M-V-ESG trade-offs.
  • Implications: Curvature reveals the extent of ESG integration in portfolios and markets.

M-V-ESG Analysis

3D Optimization setting:

Minimize \( W^T \Sigma W \)

Subject to:

  • \( W^T E = E_p \)
  • \( W^T ESG = ESG_p \)
  • \( W^T 1 = 1 \)

Analytical solution:

\( V_p = \mu^T M^{-1} \mu \)

Where:

\[ \mu = \begin{bmatrix} E_p \\ ESG_p \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} \]

\[ M = \begin{bmatrix} E^T \Sigma^{-1} E & E^T \Sigma^{-1} ESG & E^T \Sigma^{-1} 1 \\ ESG^T \Sigma^{-1} E & ESG^T \Sigma^{-1} ESG & ESG^T \Sigma^{-1} 1 \\ 1^T \Sigma^{-1} E & 1^T \Sigma^{-1} ESG & 1^T \Sigma^{-1} 1 \end{bmatrix} \]

M-V-ESG Efficient surface is generated by:

\[ P(E_p, ESG_p) = \begin{bmatrix} \mu^T M^{-1} \mu \\ E_p \\ ESG_p \end{bmatrix} \]

Curvature

  • Initial MV Efficient portfolio
  • Direction: FE, FV
  • FV contour is generated using:
\[ f(E_p, ESG_p) = M_{11}^{-1} E_p^2 + M_{22}^{-1} ESG_p^2 + 2M_{12}^{-1} E_p ESG_p + 2M_{13}^{-1} E_p + 2M_{23}^{-1} ESG_p + M_{33}^{-1} - \sigma^{2*} \]
  • FE contour is generated using:
\[ f(V_p, ESG_p) = M_{11}^{-1} {E^*}^2 + M_{22}^{-1} ESG_p^2 + 2M_{12}^{-1} E^* ESG_p + 2M_{13}^{-1} E^* + 2M_{23}^{-1} ESG_p + M_{33}^{-1} - V_p \]

Curvature from Goldman (2005):

\[ \kappa = \frac{(-F_y F_x) \begin{bmatrix} F_{xx} & F_{xy} \\ F_{yx} & F_{yy} \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} -F_y \\ F_x \end{bmatrix}}{(F_x^2 + F_y^2)^{3/2}} \]

Conclusions

  • Proposes a measure for ESG integration in relation to return-risk trade-offs.
  • Applies curvature analysis to four major global markets.
  • GMV and MSR portfolios can improve ESG scores with minimal impact on return and risk.
  • Curvature declines with higher risk, favoring low-risk-aversion investors for ESG gains.
